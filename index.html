The Big 5

African Buffalo

Considered one of Africa's most dangerous animals due to its unpredictable and defensive nature. These powerful bovines can be found in large herds, creating an imposing sight across the plains of Kenya.

  • Weight: ~1,100-2,000 lbs
  • Height: ~4-5.6 ft (at the shoulder)
  • Habitat: Savannas, woodlands, and floodplains

African Elephant

The largest land animal on Earth, African elephants are highly intelligent and social creatures. They live in close-knit family herds led by a matriarch and are known for their strong memory and complex communication.

  • Weight: ~13,000 lbs
  • Height: ~10-13 ft (at the shoulder)
  • Habitat: Forests, savannas, and grasslands

Lion

The "King of the Savannah" and the only cat species that lives in groups, called prides. Lions are apex predators, with lionesses doing most of the hunting to provide for the pride.

  • Weight: ~330-550 lbs
  • Height: ~3.9 ft (at the shoulder)
  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas

Leopard

The most elusive of the big cats. Leopards are known for their incredible stealth and powerful climbing ability, often dragging their prey up into trees to keep it safe from other predators.

  • Weight: ~65-200 lbs
  • Height: ~2 ft (at the shoulder)
  • Habitat: Forests, savannas, and mountains

Rhinoceros

An ancient and powerful herbivore, the rhinoceros is a critically endangered species. Their horn is not bone but compressed keratin, the same substance as human fingernails, making them a target for poaching.

  • Weight: ~1,800-3,000 lbs
  • Height: ~4-6 ft (at the shoulder)
  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas

Birds

Lilac-breasted Roller

Known for its vibrant plumage, this bird is famous for its acrobatic courtship display. Males fly high into the air and then descend in a fast, rocking flight, showing off their stunning colors.

  • Weight: ~3.7 oz
  • Length: ~14 inches
  • Habitat: Open woodlands and savannas

Ostrich

The world's largest flightless bird, Ostriches are incredibly fast runners and can deliver a powerful, forward-facing kick with their strong legs to defend themselves from predators like lions. They have the largest eye of any land animal.

  • Weight: ~200-300 lbs
  • Height: ~6-9 ft
  • Habitat: Savannas and semi-arid plains

Greater Flamingo

The largest and most widespread species of flamingo, often seen in vast, pink flocks around Kenya's alkaline lakes. Their distinctive color comes from the carotenoid pigments in the algae and crustaceans they eat.

  • Height: ~4.7 ft
  • Weight: ~4.4-8.8 lbs
  • Habitat: Shallow lakes, lagoons, and estuaries

Secretarybird

A large, terrestrial bird of prey native to Africa. It is famous for its long, powerful legs, which it uses to stomp its prey—often venomous snakes—to death.

  • Height: ~4.3 ft
  • Weight: ~5-9 lbs
  • Habitat: Open grasslands and savannas

Marine Life

Green Sea Turtle

A majestic resident of Kenya's coast, these turtles are known for their incredible, long-distance migrations between feeding grounds and nesting beaches. They are named for the green fat under their shell, caused by their herbivorous diet.

  • Weight: ~300-400 lbs
  • Length: ~3-5 ft
  • Habitat: Coastal waters and estuaries

Humpback Whale

A gentle giant of the Indian Ocean, Humpback whales are famous for their acrobatic breaches and their long, complex "songs." Each whale has a unique pattern of markings on its tail flukes, acting as a unique fingerprint.

  • Weight: ~55,000-88,000 lbs
  • Length: ~40-50 ft
  • Habitat: Oceans, migrating to coastal areas to breed

Dolphin

Highly intelligent and social marine mammals, dolphins live in groups called pods. They are known for their playful behavior, including leaping out of the water and riding the bow waves of boats. They communicate using a complex series of clicks and whistles.

  • Weight: ~300-500 lbs
  • Length: ~6-13 ft
  • Habitat: Oceans and coastal waters

Whale Shark

The largest known living fish species. These gentle giants are filter feeders, consuming plankton and small fish by swimming with their mouths wide open. They are non-aggressive and a popular subject for marine tourism.

  • Weight: ~20,000-40,000 lbs
  • Length: ~30-40 ft
  • Habitat: Tropical oceans
